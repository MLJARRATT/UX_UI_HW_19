UX Skills

User Research

My favorite part of UX is the user research phase. I love the design process that goes into conducting research and presenting findings. I enjoy using affinity diagrams and storyboarding to name a few! Another key aspect of User Research are user interviews. I approach all my interviews as if talking with a friend. I believe it helps the interviewee to relax and open up about the subject. I always try to ask open ended questions and dig deeper into the “why”.

User Interface Design

The most important trait a UX designer needs is the ability to empathize with the user. The user is who we are advocating for and we must not be afraid to speak up! I like to think deeply and put myself in the user’s shoes to create a world where I can think as if I am the user. All of this combined is what makes a meaningful user interface. It should be intuitive to the designer after having done successful research.

Web Development

One of the most rewarding aspects of UX/UI design to me is front-end web development. It gives me so much room to be creative and track the progress of my site easily. This is arguably the most important part of the design process as this is what the user will see and directly interact with.

Case Studies

Mobile App

Background: Our design bootcamp was given the task of finding any common problem and designing a solution around it.

Problem Statement: We have observed that college counselors and parents are not providing the best insight for prospective colle students in their college search. Students are in need of a tool that will help identify colleges that best suit their social, educational, and financial needs.

Solution: A college research app that will tailor results to each individual based on their customized profile and CliftonStrengths talen assessment results.

Website Redesign

Background: Our design bootcamp was given the task of redesigning a nonforprofit website. Our group chose Make-A-Wish as the website was newly redone and presented a challenge as well as the deep connection to the foundation as a group.

Problem Statement: Wish recipients often feel that communication with wish coordinators is subpar and need an easier way to receive updates about their children's wishes or qualification status.

Solution: To create a user login where wish recipients can view a calendar of upcoming events, chat with their assigned wish coordinators, and track the progess of their wish throughour the wish-granting process.

Website Redesign

Background: Our design bootcamp was given the task of redesigning a government website as they are typically lacking in user experience and interface. The reason for choosing the U.S. Department of Energy was primarliy due to the lack of proper navigation as well as the first impression of the website as a whole.

Problem Statement: The U.S. Department of Energy website has poor navigation which makes it difficult for the user to find the information needed. The user can be easily confused and immediately becomes lost when searching for specific documents.

Solution: Create a streamlined process for finding information by updating the navigation and simplifying the website as a whole.
